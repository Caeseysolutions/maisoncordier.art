About this work

The story behind
Coastal Grasses

Tall stems bending in the coastal wind. Warm ochres, soft browns, pale creams and deep blues — built up in loose, energetic layers that capture not just the look of the landscape but the feeling of standing inside it.

Kirsteen painted this work from direct observation of Scotland's wild coastal edges — the kind of place where land, water and sky stop being separate things. The seed heads and grasses are rendered with a lightness that suggests movement even in a still room.

The palette shifts as light changes: cooler in morning, warmer by afternoon. This is a painting that rewards living with — the longer you look, the more it gives back.

Painted in mixed media on canvas with edges painted and ready to hang. No framing required. One work, one home. When it leaves this collection, it leaves permanently.
